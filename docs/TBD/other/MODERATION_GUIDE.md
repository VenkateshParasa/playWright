## Moderator Responsibilities

### Primary Duties

- Monitor community content and activity
- Enforce community guidelines
- Remove inappropriate content
- Issue warnings and suspensions
- Respond to user reports
- Mediate conflicts
- Support positive community culture
- Document moderation actions

### Time Commitments

**Study Group Moderators:**
- Check group daily
- Respond within 24 hours
- Weekly activity review
- Monthly group health check

**Forum Moderators:**
- Monitor assigned categories
- Review flagged content within 12 hours
- Respond to urgent reports immediately
- Weekly moderation report

**Platform Administrators:**
- Overall system oversight
- Handle escalated issues
- Review moderation decisions
- Update policies as needed

## Content Moderation

### Review Process

1. **Receive Report/Alert**
   - Check moderation queue
   - Review flagged content
   - Read report details
   - Check user history

2. **Assess Violation**
   - Identify specific guideline violation
   - Determine severity
   - Check for repeat offenses
   - Consider context

3. **Take Action**
   - Remove content if necessary
   - Issue appropriate warning
   - Document decision
   - Notify user

4. **Follow Up**
   - Monitor user behavior
   - Track appeal if filed
   - Update moderation log
   - Report to team

### Violation Categories

#### Spam (Severity: Low to High)

**Indicators:**
- Repetitive posts
- Promotional content
- Off-topic links
- Multiple URL posts
- Copied content

**Actions:**
- First offense: Remove content + informal warning
- Repeat offense: 24-hour mute + formal warning
- Continued: 7-day suspension
- Severe spam: Permanent ban

#### Harassment (Severity: High)

**Indicators:**
- Personal attacks
- Targeted negativity
- Doxxing attempts
- Threats
- Stalking behavior

**Actions:**
- Immediate content removal
- Formal warning minimum
- Often immediate suspension
- Permanent ban for severe cases
- Legal notification if warranted

#### Inappropriate Content (Severity: Medium to High)

**Indicators:**
- NSFW material
- Violent content
- Offensive language
- Discriminatory content
- Graphic material

**Actions:**
- Immediate removal
- Formal warning
- Suspension based on severity
- Permanent ban for egregious violations

#### Misinformation (Severity: Medium)

**Indicators:**
- Factually incorrect information
- Misleading content
- Dangerous advice
- Unverified claims as fact

**Actions:**
- Add correction note
- Request source/correction
- Remove if harmful
- Warn for repeated offenses

#### Policy Violations (Severity: Varies)

**Indicators:**
- Multiple accounts
- Vote manipulation
- Gaming the system
- Academic dishonesty
- Terms of service violations

**Actions:**
- Case-by-case assessment
- Account suspension
- Feature restrictions
- Permanent ban for serious cases

### Content Removal Guidelines

**Remove Immediately:**
- Illegal content
- Doxxing/personal information
- Explicit harassment
- Graphic violence
- NSFW material
- Credible threats

**Remove After Review:**
- Spam
- Off-topic content
- Duplicate posts
- Low-quality content
- Policy violations

**Edit/Correct:**
- Minor formatting issues
- Broken links
- Misleading titles
- Technical errors

**Leave But Monitor:**
- Heated but civil debates
- Edge cases
- Borderline content
- Controversial opinions

## User Management

### Warning System

#### Level 1: Informal Warning

**When to Use:**
- First minor offense
- Unclear guidelines
- Honest mistake
- Borderline behavior

**Process:**
- Send private message
- Explain guideline
- No formal record
- Friendly tone

**Template:**
```
Hi [Username],

I noticed [specific behavior]. While this isn't a formal warning,
I wanted to let you know that [explanation of guideline].

Please [suggested action]. Thanks for being part of our community!

Best regards,
[Moderator Name]
```

#### Level 2: Formal Warning

**When to Use:**
- Clear guideline violation
- Second offense
- Moderate severity
- After informal warning

**Process:**
- Document in system
- Send warning message
- Add to user record
- Set review period

**Template:**
```
Subject: Formal Community Warning

Dear [Username],

This is a formal warning regarding [specific violation] on [date].

Violation: [Description]
Guideline Violated: [Section]
Action Taken: [Content removed/edited]

Please review our Community Guidelines at [URL].
Future violations may result in suspension.

If you have questions, reply to this message.

[Moderator Name]
```

#### Level 3: Final Warning

**When to Use:**
- After formal warning
- Serious violation
- Pattern of behavior
- Before suspension

**Process:**
- Official documentation
- Notify admin team
- Explain consequences
- Set strict probation

**Template:**
```
Subject: Final Warning - Account at Risk

Dear [Username],

This is your final warning regarding violations of our Community
Guidelines.

Previous Warnings: [List]
Current Violation: [Description]
Date: [Date]

Your account is now under review. Any further violations will
result in immediate suspension or permanent ban.

You have 48 hours to respond if you wish to appeal this decision.

[Moderator Name]
[Admin Contact]
```

### Suspension Procedures

#### Temporary Suspension

**Duration Options:**
- 24 hours (minor repeat offenses)
- 3 days (moderate violations)
- 7 days (serious violations)
- 14 days (very serious violations)
- 30 days (severe/repeat violations)

**Process:**
1. Document all violations
2. Notify user of suspension
3. Set suspension end date
4. Add to moderation log
5. Send unsuspension notification

#### Permanent Ban

**Criteria:**
- Multiple suspensions
- Severe violations
- Threat to community
- Terms of service violation
- No possibility of rehabilitation

**Process:**
1. Admin approval required
2. Comprehensive documentation
3. Final notification sent
4. Account disabled
5. IP address flagged (if warranted)
6. Content review and cleanup

## Spam Detection

### Automated System

The platform uses automated spam detection:

**Spam Indicators:**
- Keyword matching (configured list)
- URL count (>5 in single post)
- Repeated characters
- All caps content
- Excessive special characters
- Copy-paste detection
- Multiple posts same content

**Spam Score:**
- 0-30: Low risk (no action)
- 31-50: Medium risk (review recommended)
- 51-75: High risk (hold for moderation)
- 76-100: Very high risk (auto-block)

### Manual Review

Check posts flagged as potential spam:

1. Review content context
2. Check user history
3. Verify if legitimate
4. Whitelist if false positive
5. Remove if confirmed spam
6. Update spam filters

### Common False Positives

- Technical documentation with URLs
- Code examples with special characters
- Legitimate resource sharing
- Tutorial content
- Multi-part answers

## Conflict Resolution

### Mediation Steps

1. **Assessment**
   - Review all sides
   - Check conversation history
   - Identify core issue
   - Determine severity

2. **Initial Contact**
   - Message parties separately
   - Gather perspectives
   - Explain guidelines
   - Set expectations

3. **Mediation**
   - Facilitate dialogue
   - Keep discussion civil
   - Find common ground
   - Propose solutions

4. **Resolution**
   - Document agreement
   - Monitor compliance
   - Follow up
   - Close case

### De-escalation Techniques

**Stay Neutral:**
- Don't take sides
- Focus on facts
- Acknowledge emotions
- Remain professional

**Active Listening:**
- Let parties express concerns
- Validate feelings
- Summarize points
- Ask clarifying questions

**Solution-Focused:**
- Identify desired outcomes
- Explore options
- Find compromises
- Create action plans

## Emergency Procedures

### Critical Situations

**Immediate Threats:**
- Violence threats
- Self-harm mentions
- Illegal activity
- Doxxing/swatting
- Credible danger

**Actions:**
1. Document everything
2. Contact admin immediately
3. Preserve evidence
4. Remove harmful content
5. Notify authorities if needed
6. Support affected users

### Escalation Protocol

**Level 1: Moderator**
- Handle standard violations
- Document actions
- Resolve minor conflicts

**Level 2: Senior Moderator**
- Review complex cases
- Approve suspensions >7 days
- Handle appeals
- Train moderators

**Level 3: Administrator**
- Approve permanent bans
- Handle legal issues
- Make policy decisions
- External communications

**Level 4: Legal/Executive**
- Law enforcement coordination
- Legal threats/subpoenas
- Major policy changes
- Crisis management

## Best Practices

### Moderation Philosophy

**Principles:**
1. **Transparency**: Explain decisions clearly
2. **Consistency**: Apply rules fairly
3. **Proportionality**: Match action to offense
4. **Education**: Help users understand
5. **Rehabilitation**: Give second chances

### Communication Tips

**Tone:**
- Professional but friendly
- Firm but fair
- Clear and concise
- Empathetic when appropriate

**Clarity:**
- Specific about violations
- Cite exact guidelines
- Explain reasoning
- Provide next steps

**Responsiveness:**
- Acknowledge reports within 12 hours
- Resolve issues within 48 hours
- Keep users informed
- Follow up on actions

### Team Collaboration

**Daily:**
- Check moderation queue
- Review critical reports
- Communicate with team

**Weekly:**
- Team sync meeting
- Case review
- Guideline updates
- Training needs

**Monthly:**
- Performance metrics
- Policy effectiveness
- Community health review
- Improvement planning

### Self-Care

Moderation can be emotionally taxing:

- Take regular breaks
- Don't work alone on difficult cases
- Seek support from team
- Know when to escalate
- Maintain work-life balance
- Access mental health resources
